Quick Itinerary Overview
Our affordable Tanzania Lodge Safari tour begins and ends in Arusha, Tanzania and if you are on a budget or short on time, this East African Safari is a winner.
Our first game viewing adventure is at Tarangire National Park, well known for its diversity, beauty, huge number of elephants and the tree climbing lions. The Tarangire River runs through the park and it attracts vast amounts of wildlife during the dry season, particularly from Lake Manyara National Park, which is our next destination.
From Manyara National Park we move into the highlands of the Great Rift Valley where we are treated to spectacular views over the Maasai Plains and Lake Manyara, as we travel to the celebrated Ngorongoro Crater home to Africa's Big 5 as well as an abundance of other wildlife. We then take a scenic journey from Ngorongoro Crater to Karatu.
The Budget Tanzania Wildlife Lodge Safari offers an unforgettable East African panoramic and wildlife expedition to leading safari destinations.
Day 1 Arusha – Tarangire National Park (Tanzania)
After a short tour briefing in Arusha, our Tanzania Budget Lodge Safari gets underway. Our first stop is at one of Tanzania's top Northern Safari Circuit game parks, the Tarangire National Park, named for the Tarangire River which flows through the park. During the dry season the river attracts large numbers of various animals. Considered one of the most beautiful wilderness areas in northern Tanzania, with an incredibly diverse landscape and vegetation, which sustains high grasses, dense bush and huge numbers of baobab trees, the park is home to a variety of wildlife, but is particularly well known for big cats, including tree climbing lions.
We enjoy some game viewing in the late afternoon, before heading to our tented camp near Tarangire National Park. We stay in luxury, en-suite tents with verandas that look out over Lake Burunge and Tarangire beyond. The safari camp features a swimming pool, lounge and dining/bar area raised on a wooden deck so that guests can enjoy the views. Sit back and relax with sundowners as the sun sets over the lake and surrounding bush, or sidle up to the cosy fireplace on chill nights.
Lunch / Dinner
Day 2 Tarangire National Park - Lake Manyara National Park & Karatu (Tanzania)
Leaving Tarangire National Park after breakfast we drive to the nearby Lake Manyara National Park, a scenic gem described by Ernest Hemingway as “the loveliest I had seen in Africa”. On our route to Lane Manyara National Park we pass through Mto wa Mbu village, home to 120 different tribes. We arrive at the park after a lunch stop and go on an afternoon game drive amid contrasting scenery.
On entering the park, the road takes us through a lush jungle like forest, home to baboons and monkeys, before opening on to a grassy floodplain. Large buffaloes, wildebeest, giraffes and herds of zebra congregate on the grassy plains. Further inland the legendary tree climbing lions and elephant can be found in a narrow belt of woodland. As well as the many animals, Manyara is home to prolific bird life and is famous for the huge flocks of flamingos along the lake shore.
Leaving Lake Manyara Park we drive through the Great Rift Valley heading for the richly vegetated crater highlands. The views from the highlands over the Masai Plains and Lake Manyara are spectacular and a must stop photo opportunity. We are on our way to Africa's Garden of Eden, the Ngorongoro Crater, but spend tonight just outside the Conservation Area in a tranquil, eco friendly lodge. Each en-suite chalet features a fireplace and private verandah for our comfort.
Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner
Day 3 Ngorongoro Crater – Karatu (Tanzania)
We enjoy a hearty breakfast at our lodge and then head for Ngorongoro Crater where we descend the steep crater walls to explore this wilderness teeming with wildlife. The Big 5 may be found at Ngorongoro Crater and it boasts one of the highest concentration of lions in the world. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Ngorongoro is a huge caldera 18 km in diameter with walls as high as 600 metres. The fertile crater supports a variety of wildlife, including elephants, monkeys, hyena, jackal and herds of zebra, wildebeest, buffalo and other antelope species, in diverse habitats.
We picnic in the crater and after an exciting day of game viewing we leave the Ngorongoro Crater Conservation area and return to our peaceful, eco lodge.
Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner
Day 4 Karatu – Arusha (Tanzania)
On the last day of our Tanzania Wildlife Lodge Safari we breakfast late and then depart the crater highlands, descending into the Rift Valley. Travelling through some amazing Tanzanian landscapes, we return to Arusha where our memorable, wildlife safari ends.
